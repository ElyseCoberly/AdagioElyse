Methodology

The method that I use in all of my classes is based off of Music Learning Theory. In MLT the focus is on auditation. “Audiation is the foundation of musicianship. It takes place when we hear and comprehend music for which the sound is no longer or may never have been present. One may audiate when listening to music, performing from notation, playing “by ear,” improvising, composing, or notating music” (http://giml.org/mlt/audiation/)

Along with lots of repetition of our songs and chants, we spend time with patterns. Patterns are like the building blocks of music. Patterns focus only on rhythm or tonality but not both. Each quality is isolated so students can focus solely on that. It is not expected at the beginning that students will even respond to patterns, but as they become more familiar with them, random responses will turn into planned responses and soon they will be echoing and then creating their own patterns.

Birth to 18 months

In this class children will begin to explore the wonders of music. With parent assistance we will learn songs and chants, listen to music and move! Students will also have the opportunity to explore shakers, rain sticks and scarves as we listen and move to music. At this age, participation involves mostly just listening and observing as students take in everything around them. Students will listen as we practice patterns given by me and imitated by their adults.

18 months - 3 years

In this class I start to encourage a bit more participation and following directions. As with the littlest ones, parents are involved in this class as well. We will listen, learn songs and chants, and move as we learn. We will explore bean bags, scarves, shakers and more! Students will listen and begin responding to patterns given by me and imitated by their adults.

3 - 5 years

In this class students attend class on their own. Participation is expected at whatever stage your child is at. In this class we do a lot of movement to recordings as well as songs and chants that I will perform. Students will have the opportunity to explore movement to music with balls, bean bags, rain sticks and more! We will work on listening and imitating patterns as well.
